Fire Fighting

@explicitHints true

Welcome

When a fire gets large enough, it can create its own weather system and bring its own rain.

Until that happens, teams rely on firetrucks and aircraft to keep wildfires from getting out of control.

  • :binoculars: Try your project in the game window.

5. Waterproof

Before the water can weaken your fire, you have to set the fire's strength.

  • :paper plane: From ||sprites:Sprites||, snap 
    sprites.set_flame_strength(location, 5)

into the
||sprites:on fire created at [location]||
container already in your workspace.

~ tutorialhint

sprites.on_fire_created(function (location) {
    scene.createParticleEffectAtLocation(location, effects.fire)
    //@highlight
    sprites.set_flame_strength(location, 5)
})

6. Drench It

Now that each new fire has a strength of 5, we can weaken the fires each time they are hit by water.

Next, we need to destroy each water sprite when it overlaps with the fire.

  • :tree: From ||scene:Scene||, drag
scene.onOverlapTile(SpriteKind.Water, assets.tile`transparency16`, function (sprite, location) {
sprites.change_flame_strength_by(location, -1)
sprite.destroy() })

into an empty area of the workspace.

  • :mouse pointer: Click the checkered square and choose the tree fire tile.

~ tutorialhint

scene.onOverlapTile(SpriteKind.Water, assets.tile`tree fire`, function (sprite, location) {
    sprite.destroy()
    sprites.change_flame_strength_by(location, -1)
})

7. Extinguished

Right now, when the strength of the fire reaches 0, nothing happens.
Let's change that.

  • :paper plane: From ||sprites:Sprites||, drag
sprites.on_fire_destroyed(function (location) {
    scene.clearParticleEffectsAtLocation(location)
})

into an empty area of the workspace.

~ tutorialhint

sprites.on_fire_destroyed(function (location) {
    scene.clearParticleEffectsAtLocation(location)
})

8. Burned Tiles

  • :tree: To show that the fire has finished burning, click ||scene:Scene|| and add
    tiles.setTileAt(location, assets.tile`transparency16`)

into the end of the
||sprites(noclick):on fire destroyed at [location]||
container that's already in the workspace.

  • :mouse pointer: Click the checkered square and select the burnt tree.

~ tutorialhint

sprites.on_fire_destroyed(function (location) {
    scene.clearParticleEffectsAtLocation(location)
//@highlight
    tiles.setTileAt(location, assets.tile`burnt tree`)
})

9. Test Again

  • :binoculars: Try your game.

You should be able to put out the four random fires by spraying water with the (A) button or space bar.
